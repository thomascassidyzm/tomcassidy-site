# Diagram & affordance taxonomy — what the engine needs to show

The two books share one DNA: every teaching unit is a *named tool* (a "Visualgorithm") = a slot of explanatory prose plus ONE diagram plus a fill-in/teach-back activity. Across ~470 pages of *Architect Your Learning* and *Reasonable Eating*, the **distinct sorts of diagrams are few and recur relentlessly** — the engine's leverage is to build a small set of parameterised primitives, not a long catalogue of one-offs. Crucially, most "Diagram" slots in the Architect workbook are **unbuilt placeholders**: the books *describe* interactive figures in prose and never render them. That production gap is exactly what the engine fills.

---

## 1. The taxonomy

### State / position
**What it shows:** where you (or a food, a problem, an activity) sit right now — a slice in a wheel, a cell in a 2×2, a point on a scale, a snapshot of a system.

- **13×4 program WHEEL** (both books, repeatedly): `EAT MORE` hub; outer rail = four domains WHAT/HOW/WHEN/WHY; 12 algorithm slices with numbered pips. Drawn at least 4× in *Reasonable Eating* (Algorithms #3,#4,#7,#8,#11,#12) and narrated as the Franklin 13-virtue origin in *Architect*. Doubles as **progress tracker** — slices light up in order (#11 then #12 *added* between p183→p192).
- **2×2 QUADRANT** (the kit's NERD): NERD quadrant (Nutrition×Energy, p147–150); Body-v-Taste-Buds (p101/104); Savor-the-Flavor (Chewing-for-Taste × Chewing-until-Liquid, p158); Why-We-Do-Things (Love-Now × Love-Later, p181); the candidate/interviewer KK-KU-UK-UU classification matrix; the Decision Matrix (Design Sprint). Cells carry colour-coded verdicts AND optional numeric scores (10/7/4/1).
- **Tiered SCALE / ladder:** "Did It Grow Into Its Shape?" natural→processed 4-tier ladder (p105+); Factory→Home HEALTHIER scale + three nested sub-scales (Preparation / Cultivation / Purchase, each with a "You" marker + 5-star rating, p185–188); Gazelle Test; reasonableness/vividness ladders (Architect).
- **State snapshots / token boards:** jug triples (12/8/5), locker open/closed grid, bridge-crossing sides, pirate splits (Problem-Solving chapter).

**Affordance:** draggable marker / clickable slice / drop-a-card-into-a-cell, with a live position readout and optional score.

### Direction of movement
**What it shows:** *which way you're heading* — the load-bearing variable. The books render this almost everywhere as **hand-drawn arrows on the axes themselves** and as explicit coaching captions.

- Cyan axis arrows ("up = more nutrition, right = more energy") on every quadrant.
- "JUST SPEND AS MUCH TIME AS YOU CAN IN QUADRANT 1" with a big grey arrow at the target corner (p154).
- "eat as near the TOP as possible" up the natural→processed ladder; the bold "HEALTHIER →" arrow on every Factory→Home scale.
- "aim top-right / top-left"; the green darkest-shaded target cell.
- Funnel convergence (many questions → one answer); Known→Unknown estimation arrow; the "virus spread" and "wind →" arrows on the problem maps (direction is *load-bearing* — counter-fire only works downwind).

**Affordance:** this is **not a separate diagram** — it is an overlay: a gradient/target-glow on the "good" corner or end, plus an arrow, plus aim-coaching text. (See §2.)

### Rate of movement
**What it shows:** how *fast* — velocity, momentum, dwell-time, trajectory over time.

- **Eating-rate → insulin overshoot** (p114–120): instant vs 20-min orange juice; the body reads the *rate* of the glucose rise, over-produces insulin → overshoot. Promises a flow diagram on p120.
- "average position *moving* toward this end" (Factory→Home scales) — a remembered *series* trending right, not one point.
- Streak momentum: Seinfeld "Never Break The Chain" (stakes rise with chain length; 273-day chain is "a monster") vs 30-day reset-to-zero brittleness.
- Exponential virus doubling; the Time Timer depleting red wedge; lifespan-integrated "eat more total"; weight-by-year testimonial + belt-notch 1→7.

**Affordance:** a remembered time-series / trend arrow layered on a state diagram; rate-driven animation (a "speed" slider that drives a curve); a streak/dwell counter.

### Response / hysteresis curves
**What it shows:** two coupled curves with a gap/lag and a target band (the kit's hunger-vs-appetite meter).

- The **eating-rate insulin overshoot** is the strongest new instance — blood-glucose vs insulin, fast rise → overshoot, slow rise → tracks a sweet-spot band.
- 10-chew taste-decay (worthy food holds taste to liquid; unworthy decays to "cardboard") — two taste-vs-chews trajectories, described verbally, never plotted.
- Inverse eating-duration vs consumed-volume; hyperbolic discounting (small-now vs big-later diverging trajectories); Monk-and-Mountain two-trajectories-must-intersect.
- Threshold bands: 10%-infected virus safety line; 15-min bridge budget.

**Affordance:** animated two-curve plot with a learner-controlled input slider (rate) and a highlighted sweet-spot band / overshoot gap.

### Flow / decision
**What it shows:** branching question→answer routing you walk through.

- **The Flow-Solve™** (Architect, p143) — a NAMED, trademarked, completely BLANK decision flow that routes a problem to the right solving algorithm. High-priority unbuilt.
- Body's insulin "panic response" flow (promised p120).
- Blue Pill vs Red Pill two-path branch (same endpoint, different willpower cost).
- "Did this make you feel good? / Did you ask for counsel?" friend-test; Racehorse Test single yes/no gate.

**Affordance:** answer YES/NO/NOT-SURE at salmon question nodes → routed to green action/terminal nodes; loops back; "you're in your script" terminal.

### Linear process / step sequences
**What it shows:** ordered numbered steps with arrows, often paired with clarification notes.

- The Dinner Party Conversation 4-step arc (Connection→Results→Explanation→Action); the Funnel's Key-Point→Story→Relevance.
- Operational Algorithms checklists: Worthy-Food 6-step two-phase (on-insertion / on-chewing); Eat-For-Fun BEFORE/WHILE lists.
- Problem-solving algorithm worked ladders (Fermi Known→Unknown chains); Jigsaw 6-step regroup; juice-pour / bridge-crossing frame sequences; Reverse Learning (backward-chained); the 7-step Reason-Ability Blueprint (with a *converging* merge at Step 4); the Design Sprint 5-phase spine (Understand→Sketch→Decide→Prototype→Test).

**Affordance:** stepper / checklist you tick or fill through; reorderable cards (forward or reverse); optional per-step "Further Clarification" note; some steps run a live timer or accumulate a tally.

### Matrix
**What it shows:** an N×M grid that's *not* a 2-axis quadrant.

- Locker toggle grid (10×10 state-over-time, scrubbable); juice reversal state table; Decision Matrix (ideas × weighted criteria, auto-totalling); the NERD "proportional two-circle" ratio restatement (green Nutrition vs orange Energy bubbles sized by ratio — a genuinely **new** depiction).

**Affordance:** editable/fillable cells with auto-totals; scrub a step/time axis to watch cells flip; resize paired bubbles to build a ratio.

### Worksheet / fill-in (the dominant Architect interaction)
**What it shows:** the "interactive PDF you write on." Most Architect tools are fundamentally this — literal blank lines in the PDF (Teach-to-SIX three lanes p23; DPC four stems p26; "Create Your Own Funnel" ×2; the good-metaphor 12-item checklist; number-system mapping table; Memory Palace map; ADEPT/Feynman/Reverse Learning panels; the 5-criterion provider rubric; the 5-question "do I know…" checklist).

**Affordance:** fill-in text areas, tick-checklists with coverage meters, sort/cluster cards, multi-instance "Create Your Own ×2" templates, and **TeachBack** capture (text or audio/video record).

### Map
**What it shows:** spatial layout — routes, territories, networks.

- Memory Palace (drop items along a route); virus-spread & forest-fire island maps; the Power-of-Association social network; User Journey; jigsaw regroup people-flow.

**Affordance:** drop/annotate/sequence tokens on a spatial canvas; tag-and-cluster network nodes; animate a front advancing along a vector.

### Timeline
**What it shows:** events/markers along a time axis.

- 30-day streak track; Seinfeld year planner; Spaced-Repetition expanding intervals; Hot-Spots weekday planner grid; weight-by-year before/after; the Pomodoro cadence.

**Affordance:** tap days to fill a streak (with reset/break mechanics); drag review markers along a time axis; before/after slider.

### Other (one-offs worth noting, not a family)
Multiplicative-factor widgets (Results = Resources×Focus×Time; Time×Energy×Technique Venn; Funnel = Better×Less×More) — recurring rhetorical "pseudo-formula"; the depleting Time-Timer dial; the pan-balance simulator; the rope-burn fuse; the recursion tree / knockout bracket (binary tree).

## 2. Tom's movement lens — position ≪ direction ≪ rate as LAYERS, not families

The books make this unambiguous: **direction and rate are overlays on the state diagrams, not separate diagram types.** The same wheel/quadrant/scale repeatedly gains an arrow and a "move toward here" caption. So the engine should give every STATE primitive (wheel, quadrant, scale) three stackable layers:

1. **Position layer** — the marker / slice / point (where you are now). *Always on.*
2. **Direction layer** — a target glow on the "good" corner/end + an aim arrow + coaching copy ("aim top-left", "eat NEARER the top", "HEALTHIER →"). This is literally how the books draw it (cyan axis arrows, the grey "Quadrant 1" arrow, the green target cell). *Toggle on for coached tools.*
3. **Rate / trajectory layer** — a remembered series of past positions drawn as a faded trail + a trend arrow ("your *average position* is moving toward this end"), or a velocity readout (streak length, dwell-time-in-Quadrant-1, doubling rate). *Toggle on where the book talks about pace/average/momentum.*

Recommendation: build **one `StateDiagram` contract** (`kind: wheel|quadrant|scale`) that accepts `position`, an optional `target`/`gradient`, and an optional `history[]` series. Direction = render the target + arrow; rate = render the trail + trend. This single decision collapses three of Tom's "dimensions" into layer props and matches every drawn instance in the books. Response/hysteresis curves are the *one* place rate is intrinsic (the curve IS the rate), so they stay their own primitive.

## 3. The Architect 'tool' template as a standard content type

Confirmed across ~15 page ranges: **every named tool is one repeating unit.** Standardise it once.

**`Tool` content type (fixed, individually-renderable slots):**
`Name` (with family prefix: Conceptual N / Procedural N / Organisation N / Learning Machine N / Algorithm #N) → `What is it?` → `Why useful?` (sub-beats: where from / why use / who uses) → `How does it work?` (often numbered) → **`Diagram`** (one of the §1 primitives, data-driven) → `Example 1-3` (repeatable cards) → **`Activity / Create Your Own`** (×1 or ×2 instances) → **`TeachBack`**.

The two bold slots are the interactive surface — the **"interactive workbook on iPad, as a web app":**
- **"Create Your Own ×N"** → a writable, persisted instance of the tool's own diagram/worksheet: fill-in text fields, drag-to-place markers, sort cards into cells, build a ratio, order steps, annotate a canvas. Multi-instance ("Funnel ×2", "ADEPT Topic 1/2") = the template is instantiated N times per learner.
- **`TeachBack`** → a universal free-text + audio/video capture ("explain it back"), sometimes structured ("TeachBack including Pyramid").
- Plus a recurring **module wrap-up** unit (Reflection / TeachBack-Pyramid / Next Steps) and large blank **journaling** spreads → a free-form reflective canvas.

Everything persists per learner (save-state), and the wheel indexes the library.

## 4. Map to the kit

**Already have — confirmed used in the wild, keep:**
- **13×4 wheel** — used heavily as navigator AND progress tracker. Add: progressive slice-reveal animation; numbered pips as progress beads; configurable ring size (13/12/10).
- **2×2 quadrant w/ draggable marker** — used at least 5×. Add: colour-coded verdict legend per cell, optional numeric-score overlay (toggle — Tom is anti-formula by default), and "sort food cards into cells" mode.
- **Tiered scale / ladder** — used as natural→processed and Factory→Home. Add: a **"You" marker + named intermediate stops + 5-star readout + direction arrow** as a *parameterised spectrum-scale*, and **nesting** (sub-scales rolling up to an average).
- **Hysteresis / response-curve meter** — confirmed conceptually by the insulin-overshoot material; generalise to **any two-curve + input-slider + sweet-spot-band**.

**Genuinely NEW, worth an engine primitive:**
1. **Flow / decision diagram** (salmon question nodes → green action nodes, YES/NO/NOT-SURE edges, loops, terminal) — the Flow-Solve™ is named, trademarked, and *blank*; the insulin-panic flow is promised. Highest-value missing primitive.
2. **Linear-process stepper** (numbered boxes + arrows + optional clarification note; supports forward/reverse and a converging merge node).
3. **The Tool / worksheet unit** (§3) — the single biggest leverage; it's the spine of the entire Architect book.
4. **Rate/trajectory overlay** (§2) — history-trail + trend on existing state diagrams.
5. **Parameterised spectrum-scale with star rating + nesting** (a richer scale than the current static ladder).
6. **Proportional two-bubble ratio** state diagram (resize-to-build-ratio) — small but recurring for NERD.
7. **Token/state board** (drag tokens between containers under a constraint + running tally — jugs, bridge) and the **scrubbable state-over-time matrix** (locker grid).

**One-offs — do NOT standardise (author as bespoke content or skip):**
Pan-balance simulator, rope-burn fuse, recursion tree / knockout bracket, the multiplicative-factor Venn, the Time-Timer dial, the marshmallow/discount curve. These are single appearances; render as occasional custom widgets, not engine primitives. The Design Sprint sub-tools (Crazy 8s, Note-and-Vote, etc.) are standard GV canon left as empty stubs — treat as worksheet/fill-in instances, not new primitives.

## 5. Recommended standard set (ranked by coverage)

Build these seven and you cover the overwhelming bulk of both books:

1. **The Tool unit** (What/Why/How/Diagram/Example/Create-Your-Own/TeachBack) with fill-in + TeachBack capture + per-learner persistence. *Covers every Architect tool and every Reasonable-Eating algorithm — this is the container everything else slots into.*
2. **`StateDiagram` primitive** with `kind: wheel | quadrant | scale` + the three movement LAYERS (position / direction-target / rate-trail). *Covers the wheel, all quadrants, all ladders/spectrum-scales, and Tom's whole position≪direction≪rate lens in one component.*
3. **Worksheet / fill-in surface** (text fields, checklists-with-coverage, sort-cards, annotate-canvas, multi-instance). *The dominant Architect interaction.*
4. **Linear-process stepper** (numbered, forward/reverse, clarification notes, timer/tally hooks, converging merge). *Covers DPC, Funnel recipe, Operational Algorithms, Blueprint, Design-Sprint spine, Fermi chains.*
5. **Flow / decision router** (question→action nodes, labelled edges, loop, terminal; "answer and it routes you"). *Covers Flow-Solve™, insulin panic, Blue/Red, Racehorse gate, friend test.*
6. **Response/hysteresis curve** (two curves + input slider + sweet-spot band/overshoot gap). *Covers insulin overshoot, taste-decay, hunger/appetite, discounting.*
7. **Timeline / streak / cadence track** (fill-days, reset/break mechanics, expanding intervals, before/after). *Covers 30-day, Seinfeld, spaced repetition, Hot-Spots, weight-over-time.*

Add the **proportional-bubble** and **token-board / state-matrix** as two lightweight extras if the problem-solving and NERD content matter; otherwise primitives 1–7 are the engine.

## 6. Open questions for Tom

1. **Numeric scoring — default or opt-in?** The quadrants carry explicit point scores (10/7/4/1) yet you write "be wary of anything with a number in it." Is the score overlay off-by-default (toggle for "geeky types"), or part of the core gamified tally (running NERD score, dwell-time-in-Quadrant-1)?
2. **Rate as a first-class tracked metric?** "Average position *moving* toward this end" and dwell-time-in-Quadrant-1 imply the app *remembers a series* per learner across sessions. Do you want true longitudinal tracking (a stored trajectory the trend-arrow reads from), or just a within-session visual flourish?
3. **TeachBack capture modality** — text, audio, or video? And is the "TeachBack including Pyramid" a distinct *structured* artefact (a fillable pyramid) you want as its own affordance, or just a prompt?
4. **Flow-Solve™ scope** — is this ONE master router across all problem-solving algorithms (Minify/Reversal/Symmetry/Extuition/Recursion/Complement/Modelling), or per-problem mini-flows? It's your most-named, least-built diagram — worth pinning before building the flow primitive.
5. **Scale "good end" semantics** — the optimum corner moves (NERD best = top-LEFT; Savor-the-Flavor best = top-RIGHT; scales best = right/top). Confirm the target is always a *data-driven config* per tool (not a fixed "up-and-right is good"), so the direction-overlay points correctly.
6. **One configurable Quadrant vs several?** The same skeleton (cyan arrows, 4 colour cells, 1-2-3-4 legend) is reused for food, behaviour, and life-activities. Confirm it should be a single parameterised component (axis labels, cell contents, legend framing, best-corner all as data) — strongly indicated, worth your sign-off.
