Reason-Ability in a sentence:

> The ability to create an extraordinary life by combining reasoned thinking and reasonable action.

It's a practical philosophy. Or even better: a philosophy of practice. So, the concept deals with the human problem of getting things done. Or indeed, for most people, of not getting things done.

Most people only make 2 mistakes in life:

1. They don't THINK properly about what they actually want out of life.
2. They don't TAKE ACTION to do something about it.

Some people do, of course. Olympic athletes, business leaders, presidents and celebrities probably all thought deeply about what they wanted to do, and then did something about it.

Assuming you're reading this because you want to address these issues, I propose that there are two approaches to solving this problem:

1. Become the sort of person who wins gold medals in the Olympics.
2. Learn how to use a system that makes it easier for you.

## System Components

So basically we've got this formula for success:

> success = average resources x focus

where *average resources* = the typical resources that might be possessed by the average person in terms of money, skill, time and energy.

Here's another way of looking at it:

> success = reason x reasonable

So, you have to have a reason for doing anything, and what you do has to then be reasonable.

I can't make it any simpler than that. And as the genius physicist Albert Einstein said:

> "Make things as simple as possible, but no simpler."

## The TALK

This is about the reason behind the vision, the why.

Why do you want your life to be any different from how it is now? How do you KNOW? Can you PROVE it? I mean, to YOURSELF?

Ok, so the only way you can know that you want your life to be different is if you have some concept for your life that is possible. You have to have experienced what is possible for people at some level in order to know that you want it for yourself.

Ok, so you have a reason for creating something different in your life. Great. Can you articulate it? Can you be specific about what it is that you want? Can you TALK THE TALK?

I'm assuming that you can. You can get very clear on what you want in terms of the detail. If not, then you can use these tools:

- THE BANNISTER
- THE WEIGHTING GAME

So, work out what it is. Get VERY clear on every single detail of your vision. It may be helpful to think of what you are going to:

- **Become** — the person you become
- **Do** — how you spend your time
- **Have** — what you have created to surround you in terms of people and things

## The WALK

So, talking the talk is easy. But can you WALK THE WALK?

No, you cannot. At least, not YET.

If you COULD, then you'd be doing it already. You wouldn't be wasting your time reading this book, you'd be writing it yourself.

So, WHY not?

Well, the good news is that it's probably not your fault. You just haven't happened upon a system that was reasonable enough. You probably tried something pretty extreme, and couldn't stick to it.

Don't worry. That's > 99% of the population.

Well, the good news is that the whole concept of reason-ability is to produce things that are reasonably easy to do. Not trivial. Not automatic, but just as it says on the tin, 'reasonable'.

What, so everyone can do it?

Well, no. Not everyone. ANYONE. Big difference.

A lot of people in the personal development world talk about setting goals and no pain, no gain etc. And all that does is just create the expectation, deep down in your sub-conscious brain (the part of your brain that pays attention) that IT'S GOING TO BE HARD!!!! Well, guess what? It is hard, and that's why you don't stick to it.

## The 2 Reasons We Fail

First of all, let's get clear on this. Most of the time we don't fail to achieve our goals. Most of the time we fail to do the things we decided to do in order to achieve our goals.

It's possible to fail to achieve your goals, but it's much more likely that you only fail to STICK to your plan. In all my years of experience working with thousands of people from all over the world, I've never encountered anyone who stuck faithfully to all the activities of their plan, to the minutest detail, yet failed to hit their objective through mis-calculation.

No, it is always because they failed to stick to the plan that they drew up for themselves.

So, the big question must surely be WHY? These were goals that were surely important enough to spend time creating plans for, so why do so few people stick to the plans that they themselves created?

In my work I generally encounter two reasons for failing to stick to the plan:

1. They didn't really BELIEVE that they were going to achieve the goal, so they didn't really try.
2. The plan was UNREASONABLE: it was just too hard to stick to.

### The plan was UNREASONABLE!

They started off bright-eyed and bushy-tailed and, 3 weeks into it, they 'realised' that they didn't much want a new car/house/partner/body anyway, so it was ok to quit.

## A Reasonable Plan

So, what is reasonable? For the average person? We need to find a system with the following attributes:

- Easy to implement
- Easy to stick to — fly-paper plan
- Forgiving
- Requires no new skills
- Robust
- Fun

So, what DO we use?

We use the 13x4. Any goal you want to accomplish is broken down into 13 component parts. Each week you focus on just one of these components at a time, leaving all others to their natural chance. After 13 weeks you repeat the cycle. The cycle repeats 4 times per year, since 13x4=52.

Hey presto. You've got it.

**MAINTAIN focus. CHANGE routines.**

## The 13x4 Master Implementation Tool

So, why is it that the 13x4 is the master implementation tool? What characteristics does it have that make it so effective?

The main thing is that it is a 'low-resistance' tool. It requires a very small amount of willpower to make it happen.

I'm going to do a short digression on willpower, as I think it's something we over-estimate the importance of in most populist thinking in personal development. Of course, more willpower is clearly better than less, but I am an advocate of using our intelligence ahead of time to create a system, or approach, that minimises the need for extreme willpower.

After all, we are only really interested in the results; we care little for the mechanism by which those results are achieved, provided they are within the bounds of commonly accepted decency.

Let me explain. If you had a goal to get fitter, then there are several approaches you could take:

**1. Join the gym.** This is pretty much the starting point of anyone who makes a new year's resolution to get fit. The problem is that it requires way too much consistent application of willpower. And gymnasiums make all their money out of this single feature of humanity.

## Organising

Simple is almost always best.

Let's take the creation of this book as an example. There are about a billion ways to physically write a book: dictating into a recording device and transcribing it later, writing by hand and compiling later, writing on post-it notes and then fleshing out, using a typewriter, a word-processor, a computer, using a journal-type piece of software, etc. About a hundred different ways or more, as you start including more modern technology.

So, after a few minutes of thinking I knew exactly what I wanted. I knew exactly how I would be most productive. The question was, did it exist?

Of course it did. The real question was much more subtle: given that I was only prepared to spend a reasonable number of hours researching possible writing tools, what chance did I realistically have of finding the technology that did what I wanted?

First of all I'll share with you HOW I wanted to work, and WHAT my likely processes were going to be:

1. Initial brainstorming in my 'Book of everything'.
2. Sketching out basic structure on paper — also in the book of everything.
3. Writing the content whenever I had a spare minute — this was likely to be largely on the iPhone, but with occasional decent stretches on the computer.
4. Compiling all the content into iBooks Author.
5. Exporting final print-ready copy to PDF format.

So, given that I was going to be doing the work largely BETWEEN two devices, one of my first priorities was to use a system that made syncing the work really straightforward.

Of all the possible solutions, I elected for simplicity above all: uncluttered access to the writing, with the only functionality being the ability to have everything synced between devices without worrying about a thing. Plus, being able to write offline, with the syncing continuing without any conscious effort.

The ecosystem I went for was called Writer by Information Arts. A seriously beautiful piece of kit. It basically does nothing fancy at all — it scarcely does ANYTHING, actually — but what it does, it does beautifully, with an elegance usually reserved for British sports cars.

I found IA Writer pretty early on in the search, but not before I'd tried a couple of alternatives, and although I'm sure there are bound to be some more suitable apps out there, I was really happy with the writing process as a result.

There's an app for the iPhone, iPad and Mac, and the syncing is seamless. I especially like the way it uses the iOS predictive text function, which saves a ton of time on the text entry, with one major enhancement being the cursor and frequent punctuation addition to the text entry pane. Pure genius.

So the system I've gone with won't write the book for me, but it will make it as easy as possible for me to get it done. I can perform reasonable actions such as 'pick my phone up to check my emails, browse the Internet, etc.', but instead verbalise my thoughts in a way that they're going to be easy to harvest later, when it comes to creating the first draft for the book.

## What is Reason-Ability?

So, the whole point of Reason-Ability is this:

1. It's not going to do it all for you, but it's about as easy as it's ever going to get for the average person to create extraordinary results.
2. It's not a zero-willpower system, it's a minimum-will system. You still have to use SOME willpower to make it happen.
3. It's not going to turn your pen into Aladdin's lamp, but it will be about as close as you can get to having your own personal genie.
4. It won't run the marathon for you, but it gives you a bicycle, so you can cover the distance in less time and with far less effort.

Do more. With less.

Awesome concept.
